ABOUT EVENT :

This is some information about event

THIS EVENT TESTS YOUR ABILITY TO MAKE USE OF EVERY AVAILABLE RESOURCE IN AN OPTIMIZED MANNER WHILE RACING AGAINST TIME. IT GIVES YOU A CHANCE TO EXPLORE YOURSELF AND BRING OUT YOUR CREATIVE AND INNOVATIVE SIDE TO CREATE SOMETHING MEANINGFUL FROM THE MATERIALS PROVIDED. BRAINSTORMING, INNOVATION AND FUN, MECHTRIX HAS IT ALL!.

STRUCTURE :

Round 1 :

MCQ Round + Task Round.

Part 1 :

MCQ Round (no elimination)

Part 2 :

In Task round participants will be provided with some materials using which they’ll have to design a model/mechanism in the stipulated time.Team score will be calculated based on the combined points of MCQ and Task rounds. Top teams will qualify for 2nd round.

Round 2 :

Qualified teams will compete for the title of MECHTRIX CHAMPIONS. Task will be on similar lines as the task round in Round 1 with increased difficulty. Points of 1st round will not be considered while deciding the Champion team. Result will completely depend on points scored by the team in the 2nd Round.

Rules :

All Rules Should be follow strickly :

Rule 1

No external equipment other than that provided by the organizers can be used.

Rule 2

Using equipment of other participants is also strictly prohibited (i.e. using some extra material).

Rule 3

The registered teams will have to carry their registration receipt and college ID cards at the time of the event.

Rule 4

Participants can be of different colleges/institutions. But, one person may not be a part of multiple teams for the same event.

Rule 5

The teams must adhere to the spirit of healthy competition and maintain decorum at all times.

Rule 6

Organizers reserve the right to disqualify any team indulging in misbehaviour or violating any rules.

Rule 7

In case of disputes, the decision of the organizers and the judges will be final and binding to all.

Rule 8

Any team that is not ready at the specified time will be disqualified from the competition automatically.

Rule 9

Rules may be changed without prior intimation. Participants are requested to check the MindSpark’19 website (www.mind-spark.org) regularly for updates.

FAQ's :

Some commomly asked questions and their questions are :

Q. Is it necessary to be an engineering student/a mechanical engineering 'student?

Ans.No, any undergraduate student, from any university/institute, can participate in the event.

Q. What preparation do I need to participate in the event?

Ans. No preparation is needed. However, presence of mind will be required.

Do team members need to be from same college?

Ans. No, team members can be from any branch, any year and any institution. However, one person cannot be a part of 2 teams for the same event.

Q. Can I participate in other events at MindSpark'20?

You are allowed to participate in as many events at MindSpark'20 as long as you are able to manage your own schedule.

Q. Do I need to bring anything with me on the day of the event?

Ans. Everything from tools to materials required for the event will be provided on the spot. Only registration receipts and college ID cards will be required.

Q. What if the problem is so tough that hardly anyone is able to get through, will you disqualify everyone?

Ans. Basically, this event comprises of on-spot problem statement, so we will take care that they will be suitable as per the time allotted for its completion.
